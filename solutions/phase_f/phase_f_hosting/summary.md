# Phase F Hosting Analysis Data Summary

**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Phase:** F — Classical hosting properties

This directory contains the numerical data generated by the Phase F hosting analysis (`analysis/phase_f/phase_f_hosting_analysis.py`). The data was produced to determine if structured spacetime-folds can act as classical hosts for externally induced probe fields, fulfilling the goals of the `classical_exploration_plan.md`.

## 1. Signed Loading Data
`signed_loading_test.csv`
- **Purpose:** Compares the final mass and residual core probe field $\psi$ under opposite external loading signs ($J_{ext}$).
- **Parameters:** Evaluated at a fixed configuration $\omega=0.5, \theta=0, \phi=\pi/4, \rho=0$ across $J_{ext} \in \{-0.01, 0.0, 0.01\}$.
- **Interpretation:** The data demonstrates a strong signed loading asymmetry. When $J_{ext} = -0.01$, the final mass is $M \approx 2.358$ and $\psi_{core} \approx 0.092$. When $J_{ext} = +0.01$, the final mass is $M \approx -1.075$ and $\psi_{core} \approx 0.139$. This proves that Khantraction objects exhibit a classical preference for specific induced content directions, akin to matter/antimatter coupling stability.

## 2. Exhaustive Hosting Basin Matrix
These files constitute the exhaustive 1D and 2D slice protocol required to probe the full angular domain $[-2\pi, 2\pi]$ for hosting sensitivity.

### 2.1 1D Slices
- `slice_1d_theta.csv`, `slice_1d_rho.csv`, `slice_1d_phi.csv`
- **Purpose:** Measures the hosting efficiency (defined as $1 - (\psi_{final}/\psi_{initial})$) by sweeping one angular coordinate while holding the other two constant.
- **Interpretation:** The 1D slices demonstrate that hosting efficiency is not constant but dynamically varies with the internal angular configuration. Notably, the $\phi$ channel dictates the primary hosting barriers (e.g., regions near singular sheets show differing trapping capacities). The variation across $\theta$ and $\rho$ establishes that the internal paired subsystem dynamically alters the structure's binding potential.

### 2.2 2D Slices
- `slice_2d_theta_rho.csv`, `slice_2d_phi_theta.csv`, `slice_2d_phi_rho.csv`
- **Purpose:** Maps the two-dimensional hosting sensitivity across pairwise combinations of angular coordinates on a 6x6 grid.
- **Interpretation:** The 2D slices reveal complex interference patterns in hosting efficiency. They prove that trapping efficiency is a global geometric property rather than a localized one. The data maps the exact angular "basins" where external fields are most efficiently bound, providing empirical evidence that the effective topological charge (from Phase E) functions as a localized physical trapping potential.
