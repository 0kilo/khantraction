## Most important result
The first nontrivial result is algebraic, not numerical:

```text
|q(omega, theta, phi, rho)| = exp(a(omega)).
```

That means a norm-only theory is dead on arrival as a model of angle-defined particle species. If Khantraction is going to survive, it has to survive through:
- non-norm kinematics,
- derivative structure,
- topology,
- geometry,
- or boundary-condition structure.

## Risk carried into Phase 1
The main risk is redundancy. The ansatz may contain a large vacuum-equivalent or identity-equivalent set. If Phase 1 shows that most of the angle-space map collapses under periodicity and quaternion-exponential redundancy, then the species-level ambition weakens immediately.

## Next action
Phase 1 must derive:
1. the exact redundancy structure of the angle-space map,
2. the size and shape of the vacuum-equivalent set,
3. and the first candidate invariants that survive coordinate redundancy.
