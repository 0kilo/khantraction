# Phase G Assessment — Classical Handedness and Mirror Structure

**Date:** 2026-04-02  
**Phase:** G — Classical rotational / handedness properties  
**Status:** Closed after audit refresh

## Scope

Phase G was audited to answer three concrete questions:

1. Does the ordered quaternion map define genuine classical chirality classes?
2. Are opposite angular sectors merely reflected copies, or true mirror enantiomers?
3. Is the rotational language solver-backed, or only an analytical placeholder?

## Audited findings

1. **Parity is not the chirality flip.**
   - The operator checks show that parity preserves `chi`, while `phi -> phi + pi/2` reverses it.

2. **True enantiomers do exist.**
   - The right-handed and left-handed mirror pairs remain nearly mass-degenerate while their chirality signs reverse.

3. **Handedness is phi-controlled.**
   - Theta and rho slices keep `chi` fixed.
   - Phi slices cross the a-chiral sheets and reverse sign.

4. **Rotational stability is not yet solver-backed.**
   - The old rotational table was a proxy based on rescaling one baseline mass.
   - It should not be treated as proof of spin-like robustness.

## Final reading

Phase G closes successfully as a **handedness architecture** phase.

It does **not** close as a full rotational dynamics phase.
