## 3. Main findings

### 3.1 The explicit hosting term is present but subleading

The gamma-on versus gamma-off comparison is the cleanest direct check of whether the Maurer-Cartan hosting term is carrying the effect.

The deltas are:

- `scalar`: `4.218847493575595e-15`
- `phi_sheet`: `8.371681126106978e-11`
- `phi_offsheet`: `0.004316488908030491`
- `mixed_offsheet`: `0.004316488906694005`

So the explicit hosting term exists mathematically, but most of the observed probe response is inherited from the background geometry rather than generated by a strong standalone trap term.

### 3.2 The current unloaded runs do not prove strong hosting

The localization ratios are the key diagnostic:

- `scalar`: `0.8720466979005634`
- `theta_seed`: `0.8717732154160472`
- `rho_seed`: `0.87185894017199`
- `phi_sheet`: `0.9888319340899799` but with early termination at `r = 3.382921053355878`
- `phi_offsheet`: `0.8182228010863636`
- `mixed_offsheet`: `0.8181990228063704`

All unloaded survivor anchors have `localization_ratio < 1`. That means the probe is not more concentrated in the core than in the outer shell on the audited runs.

So the current Phase F package supports probe response, not a strong trapping proof.

### 3.3 Signed loading asymmetry is real

The signed loading ladder shows a clear monotone sign dependence.

For `scalar`:

- negative loading raises the mass to `2.167485190562235` and lowers the probe response to `0.930769698007601`,
- positive loading lowers the mass to `-1.1991859381621084` and raises the probe response to `1.3993697457290926`.

For `phi_offsheet`:

- negative loading raises the mass to `5.6027902759390065` and lowers the probe response to `0.6952276171106406`,
- positive loading lowers the mass to `1.411472698310113` and raises the probe response to `1.750916228510713`.

`mixed_offsheet` follows the same pattern to numerical precision.

So opposite signed loadings do matter. But because the unloaded runs do not prove trapping, the correct interpretation is load-response asymmetry, not proof of stable hosted matter.

### 3.4 Angular response is mostly phi-driven

The representative anchors already show the structure:

- `scalar`, `theta_seed`, and `rho_seed` are nearly identical,
- `phi_offsheet` and `mixed_offsheet` are nearly identical but clearly separated from scalar,
- `phi_sheet` is special mainly because it terminates early.

The slice widths confirm the same picture:

- 1D theta width: `9.294787219640933e-13`
- 1D phi width: `0.08804633536947054`
- 1D rho width: `0.007467723133360876`
- 2D `(theta, rho)` width: `0.007467723134008808`
- 2D `(phi, theta)` width: `0.08804633205702057`
- 2D `(phi, rho)` width: `0.08804633207320292`

So the probe response is clearly phi-dominated, with rho secondary and theta nearly flat.

### 3.5 The old trap-validation claim was too strong

The old wording claimed that the Phase E external proxy had been physically validated as a trapping potential.

The refreshed evidence does not support that:

- the most responsive phi-rich off-sheet anchors still have localization ratios well below 1,
- the explicit hosting-term deltas are small,
- the exact-sheet anchor terminates early before supporting a deep hosting interpretation.

So the honest Phase F conclusion is narrower than the old note claimed.
