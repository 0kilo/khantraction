## Synthesis

Phase E asks a sharper question than the earlier phases:

> once an object has an internal structured core, what does it look like from far away?

The refreshed answer is not all-or-nothing.

What survives:
- some full-domain survivor anchors admit smooth outer tails up to the audited `r = 40` box,
- those tails can be fit reasonably by an RN-like mass function,
- the off-sheet phi-rich pair `phi_offsheet` / `mixed_offsheet` is externally almost indistinguishable despite internal differences,
- and the external footprint remains phi-dominated, just as the earlier internal diagnostics suggested.

What fails:
- the old exact-sheet `phi_dom` / `fully_mixed` asymptotic story,
- and the old “compact objects resist acceleration like massive particles” claim.

The key audit correction is simple:

- exact-sheet anchors terminate around `r ≈ 3.385`,
- so they never enter the genuine far-tail regime,
- and should not have been used as the main evidence for deep asymptotic charge extraction.

The other major correction is the gradient probe:

- tiny gradients give small, species-dependent shifts,
- stronger gradients quickly become non-perturbative,
- and one survivor branch even develops a negative final mass under the naive loading ansatz.

So the Phase E handoff is:

> external tail phenomenology exists and external indistinguishability exists, but a reliable effective equation-of-motion picture is not yet in place.

That is a useful result. It tells later phases that “external particle-likeness” is partly real and partly still an open modeling problem.
