# Phase D Synthesis — Audit Refresh

**Date:** 2026-04-02
**Phase:** D — Identity and persistence

## Purpose

This note summarizes the audited Phase D result after tracing the paper framing, the exploration plan, the inherited Phase A and Phase C evidence, the Phase D solver, and the regenerated solution package.

## Synthesis

Phase D begins from the Phase C result, not from a blank slate.

By the end of Phase C, the project had already learned three things:
- the native linear-basis runtime is angularly blind,
- the active Maurer-Cartan-based runtime is exploratory rather than final,
- and phi is the dominant driver of the observed angular splitting.

That means Phase D is not really asking whether the objects have some abstract identity in the vacuum. It is asking a more precise classical question:

> within the current exploratory runtime, which deformations preserve broad family behavior, which variables define the important boundaries, and does anything like a rigid species picture survive?

The refreshed answer is mixed.

What survives:
- scale variation is distinct from angular variation,
- local families do exist on the regular side of phi-controlled boundaries,
- the exact phi sheet is dynamically special,
- and theta/rho remain much weaker than phi in the current runtime.

What does not survive:
- there is no scale-invariant identity fingerprint,
- there is no clean evidence for discrete quantized species,
- there is no universal absolute-rigidity result.

The key audit correction is the rigidity story.

The old Phase D wording treated outer-boundary insensitivity at the exact `phi = pi/4` sheet anchor as a rigidity proof. The refreshed data shows that this was an artifact:
- that anchor terminates early near `r ≈ 3.384`,
- so varying the outer boundary never actually probes beyond the same short interval.

Once full-domain survivors are used instead, both amplitude sensitivity and outer-box sensitivity are substantial.

So the honest Phase D handoff is:

> identity is currently a local basin concept tied strongly to phi-controlled boundaries, and rigidity remains an open problem rather than a solved one.

That is a real advance. It tells later phases exactly what they may build on and exactly what they may not assume.
